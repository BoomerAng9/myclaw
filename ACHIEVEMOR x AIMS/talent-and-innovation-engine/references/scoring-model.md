# Confidence Scoring Model

## Purpose

Use this model to rank how strongly a learner, candidate, cohort, or training pathway aligns with a target role or industry pathway.

## 100-point weighting model

| Factor | Weight | What to check |
|---|---:|---|
| Practical hands-on experience | 25 | Real work, labs, projects, internships, simulations, field work, delivery evidence |
| Role or task similarity | 20 | Similarity between current tasks and target role tasks |
| Target industry alignment | 15 | Direct match to the target industry or initiative sector |
| Credential match | 15 | Direct relevance and credibility of certifications, degrees, or certificates |
| Transferable knowledge | 10 | Adjacent industry knowledge or domain overlap |
| Geography or company fit | 10 | Relevance to region, employer context, or labor market |
| Learner or candidate intent | 5 | Clear stated direction toward the target role or field |

## Interpretation bands

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 80-100 | Strong fit or great fit |
| 60-79 | Transition fit, entry fit, or developing fit |
| Below 60 | Weak fit |

## Weak-match statement

If the match is weak, write exactly:

`match is not strong; find a better match criteria.`

## Missing evidence

If evidence for role, salary, credential, or pathway mapping is missing or too weak:
- mark the field as `insufficient evidence`
- reduce the score accordingly
- explain the main missing factor in notes
